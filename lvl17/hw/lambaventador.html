The Koenigsegg Jesko is a hypercar produced by Swedish automaker Koenigsegg, first revealed in 2019.
Named after the founder’s father, Jesko von Koenigsegg, the car is designed for extreme performance and cutting-edge technology.
The Jesko is powered by a 5.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine, delivering up to 1,600 horsepower when running on E85 fuel, making it one of the most powerful production cars ever built.
With this immense power, the Jesko can achieve incredible acceleration, with top speeds that push the limits of what a road car can achieve, potentially exceeding 300 mph.
The car features an innovative 9-speed transmission called the "Light Speed Transmission," which provides faster gear shifts and greater efficiency.
The Jesko’s design focuses on both aerodynamics and lightweight construction, with a carbon fiber body and active aerodynamics to optimize downforce and stability at high speeds.
Inside, the Jesko offers a minimalist yet luxurious cabin, featuring high-tech displays and customizable interior finishes.
The Koenigsegg Jesko is a true example of extreme engineering, blending raw power with advanced technology and pushing the boundaries of what is possible in the automotive world.
