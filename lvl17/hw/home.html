when were cars envented

Cars were created in the late 19th century.
The first true car was built in 1885 by a German inventor named Karl Benz.
It was called the Benz Patent-Motorwagen.
This car ran on an engine powered by gasoline, unlike earlier vehicles that were powered by steam or electricity.
In 1908, Henry Ford made a big improvement by inventing the assembly line.
This allowed cars to be made much faster and cheaper, making them affordable for many people.
Over time, cars became faster, safer, and more advanced.
Today, cars are an essential part of daily life for millions of people around the world.




when were motorcycles envented

Motorcycles were invented in the late 19th century. The first true motorcycle was created in 1885 by a German inventor named Gottlieb Daimler.
He built a small engine that was mounted on a wooden frame, which is considered the first motorized bicycle.
Around the same time, Wilhelm Maybach helped improve the design.
In the early 1900s, motorcycles became more popular and started being produced by companies like Harley-Davidson and Indian in the United States.
These early motorcycles had simple designs, but over time, they became faster, safer, and more advanced.
Today, motorcycles are used for both transportation and recreation worldwide.
